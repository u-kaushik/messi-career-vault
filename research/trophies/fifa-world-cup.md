# FIFA World Cup trophy — vector study 01

## Identity check

- Subject: the post-1974 FIFA World Cup Trophy, not the Jules Rimet Trophy.
- Designer: Silvio Gazzaniga.
- Form: two human figures rise in spirals and support the globe.
- Material cues retained: 18-carat gold body and two green malachite bands.
- Reference proportions: 36.8 cm high; approximately 13 cm across the base.

Primary references:

- [FIFA — Silvio Gazzaniga and the trophy](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/silvio-gazzaniga-trophy-museum)
- [FIFA — The story of the FIFA World Cup trophy](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/trophy-design-history-jules-rimet)

## Drawing decisions

- Built as native paths, gradients and clipping paths; no embedded raster image.
- Preserves the asymmetric paired figures, globe-to-body ratio, tapered pedestal and two malachite rings.
- Omits the protected FIFA word mark and base engraving at icon size.
- Master viewBox: `0 0 512 512`; designed to remain legible down to the archive’s 64 px honour card.

## Commercial note

This is an editorial prototype. Before selling a set based on recognisable real-world trophies, obtain specialist advice about the trophy designs, competition names, trade dress and permitted descriptive use. Do not market it as official or FIFA-licensed.
